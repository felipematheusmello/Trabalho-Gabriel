Who is Ray Smith?

Ray Smith is one of the main characters in Jack Kerouac's novel "On the Road". He is a alter ego of Kerouac, who based on his own experiences to create the story of the book. Ray is a young writer who, like Kerouac, is in search of adventures and meaning in his life.

About Ray Smith

About

  • At the beginning of the book, Ray is dissatisfied with his life and decides to embark on a journey across the United States with his friend Dean Moriarty, who is inspired by Kerouac's friend Neal Cassady. Together, they travel by car across the country, meeting new people, experimenting with drugs, and living each moment intensely.
  • Throughout the story, Ray undergoes various personal transformations, learning about himself and the world around him. He is a sensitive and reflective character who seeks to understand human nature and find his place in the world.
  • Ray is a symbol of the Beat generation, which sought freedom, spontaneity, and authenticity. His story reflects the values and concerns of this era, marked by profound social and cultural changes in the United States.
  • In summary, Ray Smith is an emblematic character in 20th-century American literature, representing the search for an authentic and meaningful life in a world of constant transformation.
